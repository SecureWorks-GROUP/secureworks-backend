The Material

Why Architects Specify Aluminium

22+ Colours & Woodgrain Finishes

Powder-coat, woodgrain sublimation, or custom RAL. Custom heights, spacing, and blade orientation to suit your property and style.

Zero Maintenance, 25+ Year Lifespan

No oiling, staining or sanding — ever. Hose it down once a year. Won't rust, rot, warp or dent like steel. Outlasts timber by a decade or more.

BAL-29 / BAL-40 Rated

Non-combustible aluminium meets Australian Standards for bushfire-prone areas. Essential for Perth hills and coastal fringe suburbs where timber is restricted.

Costs Less Over 10 Years Than Timber

Higher upfront, but zero maintenance costs and no replacement cycle means aluminium saves 30%+ per year compared to timber fencing over a decade.

Common Questions

Aluminium slat fencing typically starts from $350 per lineal metre fully installed, depending on height, spacing, and finish. A standard residential boundary fence usually ranges from $7,000 to $18,000. We provide fixed-price quotes after a free design consultation — no surprises.
For premium properties, aluminium offers significant advantages: it won't rust, dent, or warp, comes in 22+ colours including woodgrain finishes, is BAL-29/BAL-40 rated for bushfire zones, and has a 25+ year lifespan with zero maintenance. It's the preferred choice for homes valued $800K and above.
BAL (Bushfire Attack Level) rated fencing meets Australian Standards for properties in bushfire-prone areas. Many Perth suburbs require BAL-29 or BAL-40 rated fencing. Aluminium is non-combustible and meets these requirements, unlike timber alternatives. We can check your property's BAL rating during our free consultation.
In most Perth local government areas, fences under 1.8m at the side or rear and under 1.2m at the front do not require council approval. Fences above these heights, or in heritage areas, typically need a building permit. We check your local council requirements during our free design consultation and handle any approvals needed.
