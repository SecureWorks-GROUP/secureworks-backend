Design Options

Choose Your Roof Style

Every home is different. We design your patio to complement your property and maximise your outdoor space.

Most Popular

Flat / Skillion

Clean, modern lines with a single slope away from the house. Attaches directly to your fascia via riser brackets or receiving channel. The most popular and cost-effective option for contemporary Perth homes.

Best for: Modern homes, budget-conscious

Raised Skillion

Elevated above your existing roofline for extra ceiling height and airflow. The raised design creates an open, airy feel while still providing full weather protection. Great for homes with lower fascia heights.

Best for: Low fascia, maximising height

Flyover

The patio roof spans over your existing house roof, creating a seamless extension. Independent post support means no attachment to your fascia. Catches breezes beautifully and suits homes where fascia attachment isn't ideal.

Best for: Breezeways, seamless extension

Gable

A pitched roof with a peak in the centre creates impressive height and volume. Hot air rises and escapes through the ridge. Custom trusses fabricated locally in Malaga to your exact specifications.

Best for: Federation homes, entertaining

Council Approvals & Finance

We Handle All Council Approvals

Most patios in Perth require a building permit. We manage the entire approval process so you don't deal with council.

  • Professional architectural drafting (site plans, elevations)

Book a free site visit and we'll provide your exact price — fully itemised, no surprises.
Almost always, yes. Patios are classified as Class 10a buildings under the National Construction Code, which means they require a building permit. If your patio meets all standard setback and height requirements (R-Codes), it qualifies for a CDC (Complying Development Certificate) — typically approved in 1–3 weeks. If variations are needed, a DA (Development Application) is required, taking 6–12 weeks. We handle the entire process for you.
Standard patios use single-skin Colorbond sheets — cheaper but they transmit heat and amplify rain noise. Insulated panels (SolarSpan) are a sandwich of steel, EPS foam insulation, and a pre-finished steel ceiling. You get thermal insulation (up to R2.4), acoustic dampening, and a flat ceiling finish all in one panel. They also span further without battens, giving you cleaner lines and fewer posts.
From acceptance to completion, a typical insulated patio takes 6–8 weeks: 1–2 weeks for drafting and engineering, 1–3 weeks for CDC approval, 1 week for materials, and 2–3 days for installation. DA projects take longer (12+ weeks). We keep you updated throughout.
Yes — most of our patios are attached to the existing house. We assess your fascia, wall type, and existing roof to determine the best attachment method. For homes where attachment isn't ideal, freestanding and flyover designs are excellent alternatives that don't touch your existing roof at all.
Over 22 Colorbond colours are available. Popular choices in Perth include Surfmist, Paperbark, Shale Grey, Dune, Evening Haze, Basalt, Woodland Grey, and Monument. With insulated panels, you can choose different colours for the top and ceiling surfaces independently.
Yes, we offer flexible finance options. We'll discuss what works for your situation during the site visit. Standard payment terms are deposit on acceptance, progress payments at milestones, and balance on completion.
The flat ceiling of insulated panels is perfect for recessed downlights and ceiling fans. We coordinate with electricians to pre-plan penetration locations before installation. Outdoor-rated LED downlights and fans are popular additions that make your patio fully functional day and night.
