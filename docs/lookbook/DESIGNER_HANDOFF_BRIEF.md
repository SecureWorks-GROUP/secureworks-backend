## PART 1: WHAT THIS IS — READ THIS FIRST

### The Product
A 32-page A4 portrait perfect-bound **laminated hard copy** lookbook. Premium sales collateral. This is SecureWorks WA's **primary closing tool** for on-site consultations.

This is NOT a brochure. NOT a catalogue. NOT a take-home piece. NOT a worksheet the client writes on. It's a **sales weapon** that stays with the scoper (salesman) and is used during every in-home consultation. Laminated for durability — it gets used daily.

### How It's Actually Used in the Field
A scoper arrives at a homeowner's property to measure and quote. Here's the exact workflow:

1. The scoper is talking with the client at their outdoor table. During the conversation, the scoper **hands the lookbook across** for the client to browse — they hold it for **3-5 minutes** while the scoper talks them through the project
2. The scoper **flips to specific pages** relevant to the client's project — pointing at roof styles, colours, pricing, etc.
3. The lookbook is **laminated hard copy** — the client can't write on it. It's a visual reference and selling aid, not a worksheet.
4. The scoper takes the lookbook back at the end — it stays with the scoper for every consultation

**The book is a scoper's tool that the client experiences.** It needs to:
- **Scan fast:** Client has 3-5 minutes of browsing. Photos sell, headlines scan in 3 seconds, no jargon. Every page must communicate its point at a glance.
- **Support the scoper's pitch:** Scoper thumbs to specific sections quickly. Section tab markers on page edges help with this. Pages must work as visual proof points the scoper can reference mid-conversation.
- **Look premium in hand:** Laminated, perfect-bound, heavy stock. The physical quality of the book IS part of the authority signal — "these guys are serious."

### The Four Jobs (Every Page Must Pass This Test)
This is the strategic framework that drives every design and content decision:

| Job | What It Means | Design Implication |
|-----|--------------|-------------------|
| **CLOSE** | Does this page help land the sale the client called about? | Decision support, pricing clarity, removing anxiety, building desire |
| **CROSS-SELL** | Does this page organically expand what the client considers? (They came for a patio — they leave wanting fencing + decking too) | Service breadth visible, bundling prompts, multi-service photos, fencing/decking shown as natural additions |
| **AUTHORITY** | Does this page justify premium pricing? Does it say "serious project builder, not just a bloke with a ute"? | Technical credibility, completed project specs, engineering references, professional presentation |
| **WIN** | When three quotes land on the kitchen bench, does the memory of this lookbook make SecureWorks the obvious choice? | Premium feel, competitive differentiation, lasting visual impression |

**If a page doesn't serve at least 2 of these 4 jobs, it's wasted paper.** Every page in the current draft has been designed against this framework. When redesigning, keep asking: "What job does this page do?"

## PART 2: STRATEGIC ARCHITECTURE — WHY THE PAGES ARE IN THIS ORDER

### The Decision Journey
The lookbook follows a deliberate psychological sequence:

```
INSPIRE (pp 1-6)     → EDUCATE (pp 7-16)    → SPECIFY (pp 17-24)  → DECIDE (pp 25-28)  → ACT (pp 29-32)
Cover, credentials,     Patio vs sheet,         Fencing types,         Quote checklist,       Final gallery,
who we are,             insulated benefits,     decking materials,     investment/pricing,    visual summary,
featured project,       how panels work,        colours, add-ons       testimonials,          service area,
patio gallery           roof styles, colours,                          process timeline       back cover
                        pool pavilions,
                        fencing section
```

**Do not rearrange the page order without understanding this flow.** The sequence is designed to:
1. Hook emotionally first (aspirational photos)
2. Educate and build authority (technical but accessible)
3. Move into decision-making (colours, materials, specs)
4. Remove anxiety (pricing transparency, process, reviews)
5. Commit (visual summary, service area, contact info)

### Cross-Sell Architecture
The cross-sell strategy is threaded throughout — it's not a single page, it's a system:

| Page | Cross-Sell Mechanism | How It Works |
|------|---------------------|-------------|
| P3 (Who We Are) | Service tags row | Client reads "Patios \| Fencing \| Decking \| Blinds \| Screening \| Outdoor Kitchens \| Lighting \| Fans" — sees breadth immediately |
| P4-5 (Featured Project) | Callout tags on photo | Labels pointing to patio roof, decking, lighting, kitchen etc. in ONE photo — "everything works together" |
| P7 (Insulated vs Sheet) | Comparison page | Steers toward premium insulated option without trashing the cheaper one |
| P11 (Patio Colours) | Coordination line | "Your patio colour can coordinate with your fencing" — plants the fencing idea |
| P13-16 (Fencing Section) | Dedicated section for a "secondary" service | Client came for a patio but sees fencing has its own professional section — "same team, same standards" |
| P20 (Fence Colours) | "Match Your Fence to Your Patio" callout | Direct prompt to bundle |
| P24 (Add-ons) | Ideas & Inspiration grid | Blinds, outdoor kitchens, screening, astroturf — each is $3K-$40K+ additional revenue |
| P27 (Investment Guide) | "Complete Backyard Transformation" card | After seeing individual prices, client sees the bundle concept and thinks "do it all at once" |
| P30 (Visual Summary) | All options displayed on one page | Scoper walks client through choices — roof style, panel thickness, fencing, decking — everything on one spread. Client sees the full scope of what's available. |

**Key principle:** The cross-sell should never feel pushy. It's architectural — the client discovers the breadth of services naturally as they browse. The scoper doesn't need to pitch fencing; the lookbook plants the seed.

## PART 4: BRAND SYSTEM

### Colours (EXACT VALUES — use these precisely)

| Name | HEX | Use |
|------|-----|-----|
| SecureWorks Orange | **#F15A29** | Accent ONLY — section labels, thin divider lines, CTA buttons, tag pills, numbered circles. **NEVER as a background fill or large panel.** |
| Dark Dusty Blue | **#293C46** | All headings. Use instead of pure black. Back cover background (the ONE dark page). |
| Mid Dusty Blue | **#4C6A7C** | Secondary text, captions, labels, supporting elements |
| White | **#FFFFFF** | Primary page backgrounds. Text on dark elements. |
| Warm Light Grey | **#F8F6F3** | Subtle background for text-heavy pages. Softens without losing the clean feel. |
| Near Black | **#323232** | Body copy only. NOT for headings. |

### Colour Rules
- **No pure black (#000000)** anywhere in headings — always use Dark Dusty Blue
- **No generic greys** — use the blue-toned system
- **Orange is accent only** — thin lines, small tags, section labels, CTA buttons. Never fill a large area with orange.
- **White backgrounds throughout** — only ONE dark page (back cover, P32)
- Body text near-black (#323232) is fine for paragraphs

### Typography
- **Headings:** Helvetica Bold, 24-32pt, Dark Dusty Blue
- **Section labels:** Helvetica Bold, ALL CAPS, tracked wide, 9-11pt, SecureWorks Orange
- **Body:** Helvetica Regular, 11-12pt minimum (MUST be readable at arm's length — the client is holding this at a table, not a phone screen)
- **Captions:** Helvetica Regular, 9-10pt, Mid Dusty Blue
- Web fallback: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif

### Design Language
- **Photography dominates.** Target 60-80%+ photo area on most pages. Let the photos sell.
- **Clean, generous whitespace.** This is premium — think James Hardie Design Handbook, not Bunnings catalogue.
- **Section tab markers on page edges** — small coloured bars on the outer edge of each page:
  - **Orange tabs:** Patio section (approx pages 6-12)
  - **Mid Dusty Blue tabs:** Fencing section (approx pages 13-16 / 17-21)
  - **Warm brown/timber tabs:** Decking section (approx pages 17-18 / 22-23)
  - Purpose: The scoper can thumb to the right section instantly without flipping through

### Print Specs
- A4 Portrait (210mm × 297mm)
- 3mm bleed all sides (final trim 210×297, build at 216×303)
- 10mm safe zone from trim edge for critical text
- 10mm binding margin (left edge for perfect binding)
- 300dpi for all images
- Perfect bound (not spiral/wire-o)

## PART 5: SERVICE PROFILES — WHAT EACH DIVISION NEEDS TO COMMUNICATE

### Insulated Patios (PRIMARY — 60% of revenue)
**Pages:** 7-16 (10 pages dedicated)
**Goal:** This is the hero product. The lookbook is built around insulated patios.

**Key messages to land:**
1. SolarSpan panels = roof + insulation + ceiling in ONE panel (no separate ceiling build)
2. Up to 15°C cooler underneath — Perth hits 40°C regularly, this matters
3. Quiet in rain — #1 complaint about sheet patios
4. Spans up to 8m without intermediate posts — open, unobstructed space
5. Hidden wiring for lights and fans — genuine living space, not just a shade structure
6. Custom designed for every home — not a kit, not a flat-pack catalogue system

**Technical accuracy requirements:**
- Panel thicknesses: 50mm (R1.2), 75mm (R1.8), 100mm (R2.4)
- **75mm MUST be highlighted as "Most Popular"** — this is the scoper's default recommendation
- SolarSpan by Bondor (manufacturer). CodeMark certified.
- Roof styles: Flat/Skillion, Gable, Flyover, Freestanding
- Attachment methods: Fascia beam, receiving channel to fascia, receiving channel to wall, flyover brackets, freestanding
- Gable infill options: COLORBOND sheet, polycarbonate, louvre, open

**Differentiation:**
- vs Stratco Outback: "We're not a kit — every patio is custom designed"
- vs cheap builders: "Engineering-certified, proper footings, premium materials"
- vs architect/builder: "Specialist focus = faster, more efficient, better value"

### Sheet Roofing (SECONDARY — alternative to insulated)
**Pages:** 7 (comparison), 11 (profiles)
**Goal:** Acknowledge it exists, show it's good value, but steer toward insulated

**Key messages:**
- Trimdek, Spandek, Corrugated profiles available
- Cost-effective shade solution — from $5,000
- Full COLORBOND colour range
- Great for carports and basic covers
- BUT: no insulation, no ceiling, exposed beams, louder in rain

**The page 7 comparison is designed to upsell.** "RECOMMENDED" (insulated) vs "GREAT VALUE" (sheet) — steers without trashing. The scoper uses this page to justify the price difference.

### COLORBOND Fencing (CROSS-SELL division)
**Pages:** 17-21 (5 pages dedicated)
**Goal:** Show fencing as a legitimate, professional division — not an afterthought

**THIS SECTION IS THE WEAKEST PART OF THE CURRENT DRAFT.** The patio section has 10 rich, photographed pages. The fencing section is thin on photos and visual proof. This is the #1 area the designers need to strengthen.

**Key messages:**
1. "Same team. Same standards. Same result that lasts." — the cross-sell hook
2. Six fencing types: Trimclad (standard), Harmony (premium same-both-sides), Slat, Blade, PVC, Pool

4. Heights: 1200, 1500, 1800 (most popular), 2100mm
5. Plinths for level changes — genuine differentiator

**Technical accuracy:**
- Panel width: 2365mm (Trimclad/Harmony)
- COLORBOND fencing colour range is DIFFERENT from roofing range — 15 standard colours vs 22 roofing colours
- **Fencing-exclusive colours:** Domain, Riversand, Wilderness, Wollemi — NOT available for roofing
- Pool fencing must be AS 1926 compliant

**Critical:** Roofing colours (P15) and fencing colours (P20) are DIFFERENT ranges. Do NOT combine them. This distinction matters for technical accuracy.

- Text-focused pages (technical)
- Gallery pages (visual proof)

The current draft tends to cluster similar page types together. Mixing in more photo-heavy pages between text sections creates better rhythm.
