# SecureWorks WA — Lookbook Content Strategy

**Purpose:** Sales tool for on-site use. The salesman flips through it with the client, points at photos, walks through product choices. "This design would work well here — look at the book, visualise it. Here's what you need to pick."

**Format:** 24-page A4 portrait, print-ready. Also works as a digital PDF sent after quotes.

**Design Standard:** James Hardie Design Handbook level. White/light backgrounds, bold typography, photography dominates every page (60-80% photo area). No page is text-only.

**Brand:** SecureWorks Orange (#F15A29) as accent only. Dark Dusty Blue (#293C46) for headings. White backgrounds throughout. One dark page (back cover).

---

## Strategic Flow

The lookbook follows a deliberate sales narrative:

**Pages 1-5: HOOK** — Establish brand, build trust, inspire
**Pages 6-12: HERO SELL** — Insulated patios in depth (the core product)
**Pages 13-16: FENCING** — Full standalone division showcase
**Pages 17-18: DECKING** — Full standalone division showcase
**Pages 19: ADD-ONS** — Plant seeds for blinds, kitchens, screening, astroturf
**Pages 20-23: CLOSE** — Why us, pricing, process, final gallery
**Page 24: BACK COVER** — Contact + CTA

## Page-by-Page Content Plan

### SECTION 1: HOOK (Pages 1-5)

**Page 1 — Cover**
Full-bleed hero photo of a stunning completed patio (golden hour, wide angle, outdoor living at its best). Logo and "Outdoor Living Lookbook — Perth, Western Australia" in clean white space at bottom. First impression = aspirational.

**Page 2 — Who We Are**
Split page: content left, lifestyle photo right. "More Than a Patio" headline. Short philosophy statement: we build outdoor spaces that become the room your family actually uses. Service overview: patios, fencing, decking, and everything in between. Credibility line: "Perth's Northern Suburbs | Fully Licensed | Engineering Certified | 100+ Five-Star Google Reviews."

**Page 3 — Social Proof**
Photo strip at top (3 completed project thumbnails). Large "100+" stat with "Five-Star Google Reviews" headline. 3 real customer testimonials with names and suburbs. This builds trust before we talk product. People buy from people — reviews early in the book anchors confidence.

**Page 4 — Patio Inspiration Gallery**
6 photos in a grid. Minimal text — just suburb name and brief spec under each (e.g., "Duncraig — 6m x 5m Gable | 75mm Panel | Monument"). Mix of styles: flat, gable, flyover, freestanding, pool pavilion. This is a pure "look at what we do" page. The salesman points and says "something like this would suit your place."

**Page 5 — Featured Project Showcase**
Full-bleed photo of our best complete project. Annotated with callout tags pointing to specific features: Insulated Gable Roof, Merbau Decking, LED Downlights, Ceiling Fan, Outdoor Kitchen. Shows what "the full package" looks like when everything comes together.

### SECTION 2: INSULATED PATIOS — HERO SELL (Pages 6-12)

This is the biggest section because insulated patios are our primary revenue driver and highest-margin product. The salesman spends the most time here.

**Page 6 — Section Divider: "Insulated Patios"**
Full-bleed photo (close-up of insulated ceiling with downlights, warm evening glow). Simple overlay text: "Our Specialty — INSULATED PATIOS — Roof. Insulation. Ceiling. All in one panel." Creates a visual break and signals we're getting into the detail.

**Page 7 — The Insulated Difference (Why Insulated?)**
Split page: large photo left (interior of insulated patio showing ceiling quality), 6 key benefits right:
1. Cooler in Summer — blocks radiant heat before it reaches you
2. Warmer in Winter — retains warmth from heaters and firepits
3. Quiet When It Rains — no drumming on the roof during storms
4. Ready for Lights & Fans — built-in service channels, no exposed cables
5. Longer Spans — fewer posts blocking your view (up to 8m+ clear span)
6. Adds Real Value — a genuine extension of your home, not just a shade structure

This is the education page. Client goes from "I want a patio" to "I want an insulated patio."

**Page 8 — How Insulated Panels Work (Technical)**
Photo at top showing panel close-up or installation in progress. Simple 3-layer diagram below:
- Top layer: COLORBOND steel roof surface (weatherproof, durable)
- Core: EPS foam insulation (thermal + acoustic barrier)
- Bottom: Pre-finished COLORBOND ceiling (no separate ceiling build needed)

Panel thickness comparison:
- 50mm (R1.2) — Standard patios and carports
- 75mm (R1.8) — Most popular. Better insulation, longer spans.
- 100mm (R2.4) — Maximum comfort, largest spans (8m+ without posts)

Salesman walks the client through: "For your space I'd recommend 75mm — it gives you the span you need without intermediate posts."

**Page 9 — Roof Styles: Choose Your Look**
4 large photos in a grid, each showing a different roof style with a brief description:
- **Flat / Skillion** — Clean, modern lines. Most popular for contemporary homes. Attaches to fascia or flies over existing roofline. Best for: modern homes, simple additions, carports.
- **Gable** — Classic peaked roof. Adds height and presence. Hot air escapes through ridge. Best for: traditional homes, larger spans, grand entertaining areas.
- **Flyover** — Sits above your existing roofline. Open, elevated feel with maximum headroom. Best for: elevated decks, two-storey homes, maximising light and breeze.
- **Freestanding** — Not attached to the house. Standalone structure. Best for: pool pavilions, outdoor kitchens, garden zones, separate entertaining areas.

The salesman can point: "Looking at your roofline, a flyover would work well because..."

**Page 10 — Patio Lifestyle Gallery**
Pure photo page (95% photography). 4-5 lifestyle shots of insulated patios in use: evening entertaining, daytime relaxing, outdoor kitchen cooking, family time. Minimal captions. This is the "imagine yourself here" page. Emotional sell after the technical pages.

**Page 11 — Colours & Finishes: Your Choices**
Hero photo at top showing a completed patio with a specific colour combination installed.

COLORBOND roof & post colours: 10 most popular swatches displayed on white background — Surfmist, Paperbark, Classic Cream, Shale Grey, Dune, Evening Haze, Basalt, Woodland Grey, Monument, Night Sky. Each swatch is a circle with the colour name below.

Ceiling finish options (3 thumbnails with labels):
- **Plain** — Smooth, modern finish
- **VJ (Lined)** — Traditional grooved panel look
- **Cedar Look** — Timber aesthetic, zero maintenance

Salesman picks up physical colour samples alongside this page: "What colour is your roof? We can match or contrast. Most people in your area go Monument or Woodland Grey."

**Page 12 — Pool Pavilions**
Full-bleed poolside patio photo. "Shade Where You Need It Most" headline. Short copy: "An insulated patio over your pool area creates a resort-style space. Swim without squinting. Supervise the kids without baking. Entertain poolside without everyone fighting for the one shady corner." Callout points: Engineered for pool environments, corrosion-resistant fixings, extends usable swimming hours, no leaves in the pool.

### SECTION 3: FENCING DIVISION (Pages 13-16)

Fencing is a standalone division. This section gives it proper treatment — not an afterthought.

**Page 13 — Section Divider: "Fencing"**
Full-bleed photo of a premium fencing installation (long boundary run, clean lines). Text overlay: "FENCING DIVISION — Same team. Same standards. Same result that lasts." This cross-sell messaging is critical: the client already trusts us from the patio conversation, now we show we do fencing too.

**Page 14 — Fence Types: Pick Your Style**
4 large photos in a grid, each showing a different fence type:
- **COLORBOND Sheet Fencing** — The Australian standard. Durable, private, low maintenance. Available in standard or same-both-sides profiles. 1.5m, 1.8m, and 2.1m heights.
- **Slat Fencing** — Modern horizontal lines. Aluminium or steel slats with customisable gaps for privacy or airflow. Popular for front fences and feature sections.
- **Pool Fencing** — AS 1926 compliant. Glass, aluminium, or steel options. We handle the compliance and certification requirements.
- **Gates** — Pedestrian, single swing, double swing, or sliding. Matched to your fence style and colour with quality hardware.

Salesman: "We can do your fencing at the same time as the patio — same team, one project. What type of fence are you looking at?"

**Page 15 — Fencing: Heights, Colours & Options**
Photo strip at top showing completed fence installation.

Height options (visual diagram showing 4 height silhouettes):
- 1200mm — Front fences, pool fencing
- 1500mm — Front fences, low boundary
- 1800mm — Standard boundary fence (most popular)
- 2100mm — Maximum privacy, commercial

Retaining/plinth options: Brief note that we handle level changes with plinth retaining up to 750mm.

Extensions: Slat, lattice, or solid fill extensions available.

Colour swatches: 15 COLORBOND fencing colours on white background (3 rows of 5). Note that fencing-exclusive colours are available (Domain, Riversand, Wilderness).

Callout box: "Match Your Fence to Your Patio — Same COLORBOND colour across fence and patio ties your outdoor space together."

**Page 16 — Fencing Gallery**
4-6 completed fencing project photos. Mix: boundary fences, front feature fences, pool fencing, slat fencing, gate close-ups. Minimal captions (suburb + type + colour). Pure visual showcase. The salesman can flip to this and say "here's what COLORBOND Monument looks like installed."

### SECTION 4: DECKING DIVISION (Pages 17-18)

**Page 17 — Section Divider: "Decking"**
Full-bleed photo of beautiful decking (Merbau or composite, warm lighting). Text overlay: "DECKING — The foundation of your outdoor room." Positions decking as integral to the outdoor living picture, not a standalone afterthought.

**Page 18 — Decking: Materials & Finishes**
Split page: photos left (two stacked — composite deck and timber deck), content right.

**Composite Decking:**
- Low maintenance, won't splinter, fade-resistant
- Timber-look colours that stay good for decades
- No staining, sealing, or sanding required
- 20+ year lifespan

**Timber Decking:**
- Merbau — Rich reddish-brown, naturally durable, most popular
- Spotted Gum — Australian hardwood, beautiful varied grain
- Treated Pine — Budget-friendly option, requires regular sealing

"We handle the subframe, boards, and finishing — integrated with your patio for a seamless result."

Salesman: "If we're doing a patio, it makes sense to do the deck at the same time — one project, we build the subframe to suit both."

Pull 3 of the strongest Google reviews. Ideal: reviews that mention specific outcomes ("we use the patio every night," "the fencing looks incredible," "wish we'd done it sooner").

### Price Validation
Confirm the "From $X" price ranges are current and accurate before including in the lookbook.

## What This Lookbook Does NOT Include

Deliberately excluded to keep it focused:
- Emergency/make-safe services (different market, different audience)
- Detailed engineering specifications (that's for the quote, not the lookbook)
- Supplier names or trade details (client doesn't need to know we use Bondor vs Metroll)
- Payment terms or finance options (discuss in person)
- Detailed FAQ (save for the website)

---

## Success Criteria

The lookbook is working if:
1. The salesman reaches for it during every on-site consultation
2. The client asks "can I keep this?" and takes it home
3. It gets shared with a partner/spouse who wasn't at the consultation
4. It pre-qualifies: client understands the product, the price range, and the process before the quote
5. It differentiates: client can see why SecureWorks is different from the other 3 quotes
