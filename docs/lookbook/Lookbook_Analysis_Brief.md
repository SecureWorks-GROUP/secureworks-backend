## 2. WHAT THIS LOOKBOOK IS AND HOW IT'S USED

### The Product
A 32-page A4 portrait perfect-bound lookbook. Premium sales collateral. This is SecureWorks WA's **primary closing tool** for on-site consultations. It is NOT a brochure, NOT a catalogue, NOT a take-home piece.

### How It's Used — This Is Critical
A **scoper** (salesman) arrives at a client's home to measure and quote. During the visit, the scoper **hands the lookbook to the client** to browse while the scoper measures, inspects, and scopes the job. The client sits at their outdoor table and flips through it **alone for 10-20 minutes**. Then the scoper sits down and takes them through specific pages relevant to their project.

**The lookbook operates in two modes simultaneously:**
1. **Self-guided browsing** — Client reads it alone. Content must be self-explanatory. Visual hierarchy must guide the eye. No jargon without explanation.
2. **Scoper-directed** — Scoper flips to specific pages during the sit-down conversation. Section tab markers on page edges allow quick navigation. The scoper points at specs, colours, and profiles to guide decisions.

### What It Must Achieve
The scoper leaves behind a separate tri-fold brochure (not this lookbook). When three quotes land on the kitchen bench a few days later, the **memory of this lookbook** must make SecureWorks the obvious choice. The client should remember: "those guys had the proper book, they knew their stuff, they showed me everything."

### What This Is NOT
- NOT a take-home piece (the scoper keeps it — too expensive to leave behind)
- NOT a technical engineering manual
- NOT a catalogue with SKUs and order codes
- NOT a digital document — it's designed for print (perfect binding, coffee-table quality)

## 3. THE FOUR-JOB FRAMEWORK

**Every single page must pass this test. Use this as your primary evaluation lens.**

| Job | Question | Why It Matters |
|-----|----------|---------------|
| **CLOSE** | Does this page help land the sale the client called about? | Direct conversion — moves the client from "thinking about it" to "let's do it" |
| **CROSS-SELL** | Does this page organically expand what the client is considering? | Client came for a patio → leaves wanting fencing + decking too. This is where margin lives. |
| **AUTHORITY** | Does this page justify premium pricing and establish SecureWorks as serious? | "These guys are professional builders, not just a bloke with a ute" |
| **WIN** | When three quotes land on the kitchen bench, does this page make SecureWorks the obvious choice? | The lookbook isn't present at decision time — its MEMORY must persist |

A page doesn't need to score on all four jobs. But it should score strongly on at least two. If a page fails all four, it's wasted space in a 32-page document where every page costs money.

### Colour Rules (Check These)
- **No pure black (#000000)** anywhere for headings — use Dark Dusty Blue
- **Orange is accent only** — if you see orange as a large background, that's wrong
- **Only ONE dark page** — the back cover (page 32). All other pages should be white/light.
- Body text in Near Black is fine. Headings must be Dark Dusty Blue.

### Typography
- **Headings:** Helvetica Bold, Dark Dusty Blue
- **Section labels:** Helvetica Bold, ALL CAPS, tracked wide, Orange
- **Body:** Helvetica Regular, minimum 11pt (must be readable at arm's length)
- **Captions:** 9-10pt, Mid Dusty Blue
- Cover uses a condensed bold style at 72pt ("OUTDOOR LIVING" — Corvus lookbook inspired)

### Design Language
- White/light backgrounds throughout
- Photography dominates (60-80%+ photo area on most pages)
- Clean, generous whitespace — premium feel, not a Bunnings catalogue
- Section tab markers on page edges (orange = patios, blue = fencing, brown = decking)
- Orange tag pills on photos for labelling features (Corvus-inspired element)

## 6. EVALUATION PERSPECTIVES

When analysing the lookbook, evaluate from these five perspectives. They were chosen because each represents a real stakeholder in the lookbook's success:

### A. The Scoper (Salesperson)
- Can I thumb to any section in under 3 seconds using the tab markers?
- Does each specification page give me something to point at and discuss?
- Does the "Your Outdoor Vision" notes page actually work as a consultation tool?
- Are the comparison pages (insulated vs sheet, Trimclad vs Harmony) structured so I can walk a client through the decision?
- Does the investment guide let me anchor the price conversation properly?

### B. The Homeowner (Self-Guided Browser)
- Can I understand every page without someone explaining it?
- Is the text readable at arm's length (minimum 11pt body)?
- Do the "Best for" lines on specification pages help me self-identify?
- Does the layout guide my eye naturally through each page?
- Do I feel like these are premium, serious builders — or just another tradie?
- After 10-20 minutes of browsing, do I feel educated and confident?

### C. The Print Production Specialist
- Does every page work at A4 portrait?
- Are there any text-on-photo pages where readability might fail?
- Are colour swatches on white backgrounds (essential for colour accuracy)?
- Is the binding margin consistent? (10mm left on odd pages, 10mm right on even pages)
- Would this work as a perfect-bound document?
- Are there pages that are too dense to read comfortably?
- Is the 32-page count correct for print signatures?

### D. The Competitive Strategist
- Does the "What to Look For in a Quote" page actually disqualify competitors?
- Does the insulated vs sheet comparison steer toward the premium product without being dishonest?
- Is the pricing on the investment guide positioned correctly (not too low = cheap perception, not too high = sticker shock)?
- Does the cross-sell mechanism work? Would a patio-only client leave wanting fencing + decking?
- Does the "100+ Five-Star Reviews" positioning beat competitor trust signals?

### E. The Behavioral Psychologist (Decision Architecture)
- Does the page order create a proper decision journey? (Inspire → Educate → Specify → Decide → Act)
- Are decision points presented at the right moments?
- Does the interactive notes page create commitment/ownership?
- Is social proof positioned where anxiety is highest (near pricing)?
- Does the "Complete Backyard Transformation" bundle card create the right anchoring effect?
- Are there any pages that create confusion or decision paralysis?

## 8. KNOWN ISSUES TO VERIFY

These were identified during the build. Check if they're present in the PDF:

1. **Placeholder photos** — Several pages have grey dashed rectangles where photos are needed (fencing gallery, service area map). Count them and note which pages.
2. **Testimonials are placeholders** — The three reviews are fabricated for layout purposes. They need replacing with real Google reviews before print.
3. **Cedar Look ceiling** — Included but needs confirmation it's currently available through Bondor.
4. **Decking pricing** — $350/m² composite and $300/m² Merbau are working estimates, not confirmed.
5. **Warranty terms** — "Structural warranty" is deliberately vague. Needs specific terms before print.
6. **Font rendering** — The condensed bold font on the cover ("OUTDOOR LIVING") may not render correctly if Helvetica Neue Condensed Bold isn't available on the rendering system. Check if it falls back gracefully.
