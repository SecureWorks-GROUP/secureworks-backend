## PAGE 7: THE INSULATED DIFFERENCE

**Photo direction (left 50%):** Interior of insulated patio showing ceiling quality — downlights on, ceiling fan spinning, warm evening atmosphere. Should demonstrate the "room-like" quality of an insulated ceiling.

**Content (right 50%, white background):**

**[Label]** WHY INSULATED

# The Insulated Difference

What makes an insulated patio different from a standard tin roof? Everything.

---

**01** COOLER IN SUMMER
Blocks radiant heat before it reaches you. An insulated panel can reduce under-roof temperatures by up to 15°C compared to single-skin steel.

**02** WARMER IN WINTER
Retains warmth from heaters and firepits. The insulated core acts as a thermal blanket, keeping your outdoor room comfortable year-round.

**03** QUIET WHEN IT RAINS
No drumming on the roof during storms. The EPS foam core absorbs sound, so you can have a conversation without shouting when it pours.

**04** READY FOR LIGHTS & FANS
Built-in service channels inside the panel allow electrical wiring to run hidden — no exposed cables, no ugly conduits. Downlights, ceiling fans, and power points integrate seamlessly.

**05** LONGER SPANS, FEWER POSTS
Insulated panels can span up to 8+ metres without intermediate supports. That means fewer posts blocking your view and more usable space underneath.

**06** ADDS REAL VALUE
An insulated patio is a genuine extension of your home — not just a shade structure. It adds liveability and resale appeal. This is a room that happens to be outside.

## TECHNICAL REFERENCE APPENDIX

*This section is NOT a page in the lookbook. It's background knowledge to ensure the lookbook content is technically accurate. Include this when sending to advisor/AI for content refinement.*

### Patio — Complete Product Knowledge

**SolarSpan Insulated Panels (by Bondor)**
- Roof + insulation + ceiling in one panel
- Available in 50mm (R1.2), 75mm (R1.8), 100mm (R2.4)
- 75mm is most popular — best balance of insulation, span, and cost
- 100mm can span 8m+ without intermediate posts
- Ceiling finishes: Plain (smooth), VJ (grooved), Cedar Look (timber aesthetic)
- Built-in service channels for electrical wiring (lights, fans, power)
- Minimum roof pitch: 2 degrees

**Non-Insulated Roofing Profiles**
- Trimdek — The standard. Strong, clean lines, most popular sheet roofing in Perth
- Spandek — Deeper ribs, longer spans, bold industrial look
- SpanPlus 330 — Powdercoated both sides, from Metroll
- Corrugated (Custom Orb) — Classic Australian profile, heritage charm
- Minimum pitch: 2° (Trimdek/SolarSpan), 5° (Corrugated)

**Attachment Methods**
- Riser Beam — Most common. Brackets lift the patio beam above the fascia. Standard 400mm riser height, 150mm offset from wall.
- Flyover — Patio roof sits above the house roof. Insulated panels only. Creates gap for light and breeze.
- Freestanding — Posts on all sides. Not attached to house. Used for pavilions, pool areas, garden structures.
- Fascia/Receiving Channel — Attaches directly to existing fascia or wall.
- Wall Mount — Direct to wall. Not available for gable style.

**Frame Steel**
- Post sizes: 65x65, 75x75, 90x90 (standard), 100x100, 125x125, 150x150mm SHS
- Beam sizes: 75x50, 100x50, 150x50mm RHS
- Post fixing: In-ground concrete footings (standard) or baseplate bolt to existing slab
- Standard footing: 400x400x500mm

**Gable-Specific Details**
- Truss styles: King Post, King Post + Verticals, Web
- Truss orientation: Along house (lengthways) or away from house (crossways)
- Gable infill options: COLORBOND sheet, polycarbonate, louvre ventilation, or open
- Overhang: 0-500mm

**Polycarbonate Panels (for non-insulated roofs)**
- Brands: Ampelite Solasafe or Laserlite 2000
- Pattern options: Every 2nd sheet (1:1), every 3rd (2:1), every 4th (3:1), or custom
- Light levels: Maximum light or medium filtered

**Electrical**
- Downlights: Typically 4-8 per patio, LED, warm white
- Ceiling fans: 1-2 per patio, mounted directly into insulated panels
- Power outlets (GPO): 1-2 per patio for appliances

### Fencing — Complete Product Knowledge

**Profile Types**
- **Standard profile** (Ridgeside / Trimclad / Neetascreen / Superdek) — Traditional trapezoidal ribs. Different finish front and back. Wider pans, fewer ribs.
- **Same-both-sides** (Sameside / Harmony / Smartascreen / Good Neighbour) — Symmetrical narrow-rib profile. Same clean look on both sides. More popular with neighbours.

**Suppliers (cannot mix on same job — different panel widths)**
- Metroll (Trimclad/Harmony): 2365mm panel width
- RnR Fencing (Ridgeside/Sameside): 2380mm panel width
- Lysaght (Neetascreen/Smartascreen): 2360mm panel width
- Stratco (Superdek/Good Neighbour): 2350mm panel width

**Heights Available**
- 1200mm — Front fences, pool fencing
- 1500mm — Front boundary, low-profile
- 1800mm — Standard boundary (most popular)
- 2100mm — Maximum privacy, commercial

**Retaining (Plinth System)**
- Concrete plinths stack under panels in 150mm increments
- Up to 750mm retaining (5 plinths) with patio tube support
- 3-5 plinths require horizontal patio tubing (76x38mm RHS) for structural support
- 6+ plinths = needs a proper retaining wall, not COLORBOND fencing
- Retaining over 500mm requires Building Permit + Engineer Certification in WA

**Gates**
- Pedestrian gate: Single leaf, typically 900mm wide, requires 2x 90x90mm steel gate posts
- Double swing gate: Two leaves, requires 4x 90x90mm gate posts
- Sliding gate: For driveways, custom width
- Gate height matches fence height
- All gates use heavy-duty hardware — self-closing hinges, D-latches

**Posts**
- Standard C-channel posts included with panel kits (don't order separately)
- Gate posts: 90x90mm SHS (ordered separately, always)
- Corner posts: Standard C-channel, shared between runs (count once)
- Post lengths: 2400mm, 2700mm, 3000mm (selected based on sheet height + retaining + 600mm embedment)

### Decking — Complete Product Knowledge

**Composite Options**
- Engineered boards: mix of wood fibre and plastic
- Common brands: Trex, ModWood, Ekodeck, NewTechWood
- Colours: greys, browns, charcoals, multi-tone timber-look
- No staining, sealing, or sanding required
- Won't splinter, warp, rot, or attract termites
- 20-30+ year lifespan

**Timber Options**
- Merbau: Reddish-brown, most popular, naturally durable Class 1 hardwood
- Spotted Gum: Australian hardwood, varied grain, extremely hard
- Kwila: Similar to Merbau, darker tones
- Treated Pine: Budget option, H3 or H4 treated, requires regular maintenance

**Structural**
- Board width: 90-150mm typical
- Joist spacing: 450-600mm (depends on board type and span)
- Subframe: Steel or timber bearers and joists
- Post sizes: 90x90, 100x100, 125x125mm SHS
- Beam sizes: 100x50, 150x50 RHS
- Balustrade options: Timber, glass, powder-coated steel, wire
