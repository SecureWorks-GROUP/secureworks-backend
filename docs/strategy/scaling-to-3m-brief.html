Executive Summary: The 5 Highest-Impact Moves

SecureWorks WA currently generates ~$1.4M/year from ~93 jobs. To reach $3M, you need ~200 jobs/year — roughly 9 more jobs per month. Your conversion rate (30%) and close cycle (1-2 weeks) are already solid. The bottleneck is lead volume. Crews have capacity. Here are the five moves that will close the gap:

1. Set up customer financing (Handypay via HIA — $0 merchant fee) Adding "or $89/week" to a $20K quote delivers +18% close rate and +30% average job size. This is the single highest-ROI action because it costs nothing to set up and amplifies every lead you already get. Expected impact: +$250-400K/year.
2. Launch a structured referral program ($300-500 Give/Get) An Australian solar company generated $8.46M from referrals in one year with a $500 give/$500 get structure (38% conversion on warm leads). You have zero referral system today. With 93 past jobs as seed, even 15% referral rate = 14 extra jobs. Expected impact: +$200-350K/year.
3. Deploy AI phone answering (Johnni.ai — $150-300/month) Contractors miss 5-10 calls/week, costing $45-120K/year. 78% of callers who hit voicemail call the next tradie. Johnni.ai is Australian-built, understands trade jargon, costs $150-300/mo vs $58K for a receptionist. Captures leads 24/7 including weekends when homeowners are home planning projects. Expected impact: +$150-300K/year.
4. Scale Google Ads from $3-5K to $6-8K/month + add Facebook/Meta at $1-2K Perth CPCs are 10-20% cheaper than Sydney/Melbourne. At current CPL, doubling spend should nearly double leads. Add Facebook for top-of-funnel awareness targeting 35-65 age bracket in premium suburbs. Expected impact: +$300-500K/year.
5. Build the Grand Slam Offer (Hormozi framework) Stop selling "a patio." Sell "an extra room that adds $30-50K to your property value" with stacked guarantees (price lock, timeline, clean site, quality inspection, satisfaction). Layer tiered pricing (Essential/Premium/Signature) — contractors offering 4+ options see 52% close rate vs 42%. Expected impact: +10pp conversion = +$140K/year.

Combined conservative impact: $1.04M-$1.69M additional revenue. That's $2.44M-$3.09M total. The math works.

EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System) — What to Implement

EOS is designed for $1M-$10M companies with 10-250 employees. SecureWorks at $3M is the sweet spot. Start with these three tools:

1. L10 Meetings (Weekly, 90 min)

Weekly leadership meeting: scorecard review (5 min), rock review (5 min), IDS — Identify/Discuss/Solve (60 min). This single practice transforms alignment and accountability.

2. Rocks (90-Day Priorities)

3-7 priorities per person per quarter. Example Q2 rocks: Hire 3rd crew leader. Document 10 SOPs. Launch maintenance subscriptions. Hit $280K/month average.

3. Scorecard (10 Weekly KPIs)

Leads generated, quotes sent, conversion rate, revenue booked, jobs completed, average job value, customer satisfaction, crew utilisation, AR days, warranty callbacks.

Skip for now: full V/TO, People Analyser across all positions, quarterly State of the Company.

Conversion Optimisation (Before Adding Leads)

Your 30% close rate is at or above industry average Home improvement contractors typically close 20-30%. 50%+ can signal underpricing. The biggest lever isn't conversion — it's volume. But these tactics can push toward 40%:
Speed-to-Lead: The Data Is Brutal
  • Responding within 1 minute increases conversion by 391%
  • Leads contacted within 5 minutes are 100x more likely to respond
  • Text responses under 60 seconds: 73% booking rate. After 30 minutes: 4%. That's 18x.
  • After 5 minutes, qualification odds drop by 80%
  • Average response time across industries: 42 hours

Fix: AI phone answering (24/7) + GHL instant SMS auto-response + webchat bot. The goal: sub-5-minute contact on every lead, always.

Section 07

Customer Experience Design

The Full Journey: First Click to 12-Month Check-In

Stage 1: First Contact (Speed = Everything)
  1. Lead comes in (Google Ad, referral, call, webchat)
  2. Within 60 seconds: AI phone answers OR GHL sends instant SMS acknowledgment
  3. Webchat bot qualifies: project type, suburb, approximate size, timeline
  4. Lead auto-tagged in GHL pipeline with source and urgency score
  5. Scoper assigned within 2 hours during business hours
  6. Site visit booked within 24-48 hours
Stage 2: Scoping & Quoting (The Grand Slam Presentation)
  1. Scoper arrives with branded site kit (iPad, tape, Secure Scope tool)
  2. Listen first. Understand the dream, not just the dimensions
  3. Use Secure Scope for live 3D visualisation on-site
  4. Mention finance early: "Most clients spread the cost — no interest with Handypay"
  5. Cross-sell check: "While I'm here, noticed your fencing — want me to include?"
  6. Generate tiered quote (Essential / Premium / Signature) within 48 hours
  7. Record 60-90 second Loom video walking through the quote
  8. Present Signature option first (price anchor)
  9. Include value stack breakdown showing $9K+ in included value
  10. Include "$X/week with Handypay" on every quote
Stage 3: Follow-Up (Value-Adding, Not "Checking In")

Your 13-stage GHL sequence is already strong. Enhance with:

  • Day 1: "Here's your quote + a Loom video walkthrough"
  • Day 3: Cross-sell email (if patio quoted, show fencing. If fencing, show patio)
  • Day 5: Social proof: "Here's a similar project we just finished in [nearby suburb]" + before/after photos
  • Day 7: Finance reminder: "This could be yours for just $89/week"
  • Day 10: Genuine scarcity (if real): "We have 2 install slots left in [month]"
  • Day 14: Final value-add: "Perth council regs you should know about" or similar educational content
Stage 4: Build & Handover (The Peak-End Moment)

During Build

  • Pre-start meeting: walkthrough of exactly what happens
  • Daily/milestone photo updates via SMS or WhatsApp
  • Clean site policy: pack up tools, sweep area every day
  • Photo documentation at every quality gate (timestamped, geo-tagged)

The Handover Moment (This Is Where Referrals Are Born)

The peak-end rule says people judge experiences by the most intense moment and the ending. In construction, both converge at completion. Design this intentionally:

  1. Clean site perfectly
  2. Walk client through finished work
  3. Show before photos vs now (have them ready on iPad)
  4. Present branded handover pack: warranty card, care guide, before/after photos, maintenance schedule, colour references, emergency contact
  5. Gift: bottle of wine, flowers, or similar (~$30-50)
  6. Take photos together with the finished work (social media gold)
  7. Mention the referral program: "If any friends or neighbours are thinking about this, we have a $400 thank-you for referrals"
Stage 5: Post-Completion (The Referral Engine)
  • Day 1 post-completion: Automated SMS: NPS survey ("On 0-10, how likely to recommend?")
  • Promoters (9-10): Immediately trigger Google review request + referral program invite
  • Day 7: Review request follow-up if no response
  • Day 14: Cross-sell sequence for complementary products
  • Month 6: Proactive check-in call. Catch warranty issues. Ask about next projects.
  • Month 12: Anniversary check-in. Maintenance plan offer. Referral reminder.
  • Christmas: Card to all past-year customers. Keeps you top of mind.

Key stat: There's a 10-point drop in referral intent within the first 12 months. These touchpoints maintain the relationship.
