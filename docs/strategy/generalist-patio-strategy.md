## The Two-Site Strategy (Why Two Sites)

SecureWorks is running two parallel suburb stacks for patios:

### Site 1: Insulated Patio Specialist (ALREADY IN BUILD)
- **Target searcher:** Knows they want insulated panels (SolarSpan, Cooldek, etc.)
- **Search intent:** "insulated patio Perth", "SolarSpan patio [suburb]", "insulated patio builders near me"
- **Positioning:** Premium outdoor living. We are THE insulated patio experts.
- **Conversion path:** Educated buyer → quote request
- **Messaging:** Technical depth, thermal performance, lifestyle transformation
- **Price point implied:** $15k–$60k+

### Site 2: Generalist Patio Page (THIS BRIEF)
- **Target searcher:** Wants a patio but doesn't know what type yet
- **Search intent:** "patio builders Perth", "patios [suburb]", "pergola builders near me", "outdoor patio ideas Perth"
- **Positioning:** Trusted guide. We build ALL patio types and help you choose.
- **Conversion path:** Confused/exploring buyer → education → guide toward right solution → quote
- **Messaging:** Breadth of options, comparison guidance, "we'll help you choose"
- **Price point implied:** $3k–$60k+ (full range)

### Why This Matters for SEO
The generalist page captures the HIGH VOLUME, LOWER INTENT searches — people typing "patios Perth" or "patio builders [suburb]" who haven't decided on a material or style yet. These keywords have 3-5x the search volume of "insulated patio" terms, but they're more competitive because every patio company targets them.

The insulated site captures LOWER VOLUME, HIGHER INTENT searches — people who've already done research and specifically want insulated panels. These convert better but there are fewer of them.

**Together, they cover the full funnel without cannibalising each other.**

## Page Structure — Generalist Patio Landing Page

This is a single-page landing template (same as the insulated site approach) that gets duplicated per suburb.

### Section 1: Hero
**Headline pattern:** "Custom Patios in [Suburb] — Built for Perth's Climate"
**Sub-headline:** "From simple shade structures to fully insulated outdoor rooms. We build the right patio for your home, your budget, and the way you actually live."

**Key differences from insulated site hero:**
- Broader language — "patios" not "insulated patios"
- Emphasis on CHOICE and GUIDANCE, not technical specs
- Still confident, but more inviting to someone who's exploring
- CTA: "Get Your Free Patio Consultation" (not just "quote" — implies guidance)

### Section 2: What Type of Patio is Right for You?
This is the CORE differentiator section. An interactive or visual comparison that helps the visitor self-identify.

**Patio Types to Cover (organised by ROOFING MATERIAL, not shape):**

#### Steel Sheet Patios (Colorbond / SpanPlus 330)
- **What it is:** Steel roofing sheets on a steel or timber frame. The workhorse of Perth patios.
- **Best for:** Budget-conscious builds, carports, basic shade structures, matching existing roofline
- **Profiles available:** Trimdek, Corrugated, SpanPlus 330
- **Perth reality:** Colorbond gets HOT underneath on 40°C days. Fine for carports, not ideal for all-day entertaining areas without fans/airflow.
- **Price indication:** $$ (entry to mid)
- **Supplier:** Metroll (SpanPlus 330 — powdercoated both sides for a clean underside finish)

#### Insulated Panel Patios (SolarSpan / Cooldek)
- **What it is:** Foam-core panels with steel skins — roof and ceiling in one. The premium choice.
- **Best for:** Outdoor entertaining, extending living space, anywhere you want comfort
- **Perth reality:** 15–25°C cooler underneath than steel sheet. No condensation in winter. Quiet in rain. The gold standard for liveable outdoor rooms.
- **Price indication:** $$$$ (premium)
- **Supplier:** Bondor (SolarSpan), Stratco (Cooldek/Smoothdek)
- **Cross-link:** "Want to know more? See our dedicated Insulated Patios page →" **(links to the specialist site)**

#### Polycarbonate / Laserlite Patios
- **What it is:** Translucent plastic sheeting. Lets light through.
- **Best for:** Areas where you need natural light — over windows, next to dark rooms, pool areas where you want sun but rain protection
- **Perth reality:** Gets VERY hot in summer (greenhouse effect). Not recommended as full roof in Perth's climate. Best used as skylights/lites mixed with steel or insulated sheets. Minimum 5° pitch.
- **Price indication:** $$ (material cheap, but framing similar)
- **Honest note:** "We install polycarb lites for light penetration, but we'll always be upfront — a full polycarb roof in Perth isn't comfortable from October to April."

#### Timber Pergolas / Patios
- **What it is:** Timber frame (treated pine, jarrah, or merbau) with optional roofing
- **Best for:** Heritage homes, design-led builds, Hamptons/coastal aesthetic, gardens and pool surrounds
- **Perth reality:** Beautiful but requires more maintenance than steel. Termite treatment essential. Paint/oil every 2-3 years. Not all timber pergolas are "patios" — a pergola with battens only isn't waterproof.
- **Price indication:** $$$ to $$$$ (depends on timber species and roofing choice)
- **Honest note:** "We build timber frames and can roof them with any material above. If you want the warmth of timber with the performance of insulation, timber + SolarSpan is a stunning combination."

#### Open Pergolas / Shade Structures
- **What it is:** Posts and beams with battens, shade cloth, or climbing plants — no solid roof
- **Best for:** Budget shade, garden features, vineyard/wisteria structures, pool shade
- **Perth reality:** Not waterproof. Won't extend your useable seasons. But significantly cheaper and sometimes all you need.
- **Price indication:** $ to $$ (most affordable option)

### Section 3: Roof Styles We Build
Quick visual grid (same as what competitors do, but cleaner):
- **Flat / Skillion** — Modern, clean lines, great for contemporary homes
- **Gable** — Pitched triangle, classic Perth suburban look, adds height and airflow

- **Flyover** — Raised above existing roofline, dramatic look, great airflow
- **Freestanding** — Not attached to house, pools, gardens, separate entertaining areas

## Content Tone — How It Differs from the Insulated Site

### Insulated Site Tone:
- Expert, authoritative, technical
- "We specialise in this. Here's why it's the best."
- Assumes some buyer knowledge
- Sells the TRANSFORMATION (outdoor living room)
- Premium positioning

### Generalist Site Tone:
- Helpful guide, trusted advisor
- "Here are all your options. Let us help you choose."
- Assumes the buyer is researching/exploring
- Sells the GUIDANCE and BREADTH
- Value-for-money positioning (we have options at every budget)
- Still confident, not wishy-washy — "We'll tell you what we'd do if it was our house"

### Key Phrases for Generalist Site:
- "Every patio type. One honest conversation."
- "We build them all — so we recommend what's actually best for you."
- "From a simple shade cover to a fully insulated outdoor room"
- "Perth's climate demands more than a standard patio"
- "We'll tell you what we'd do if it was our house"
- "No pushy sales, no hidden costs, no surprises"

### Phrases to AVOID:
- "Luxury outdoor living" (save that for the insulated site)
- "Extend your living space" (that's insulated territory)
- "Perth's premium patio builder" (too narrow for the generalist page)
- Technical jargon without explanation (R-values, U-values etc. — save for insulated)
