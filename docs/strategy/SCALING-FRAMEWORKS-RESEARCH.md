## 1. Michael Gerber / E-Myth Revisited -- Applied to Trades

### 1.1 Core Framework: Technician > Manager > Entrepreneur

Gerber's central argument is the **"Entrepreneurial Myth"** -- the fatal assumption that because someone understands the *technical work* of a business, they can successfully *run* that business. Most trade business owners are Technicians who had an "entrepreneurial seizure" and started a business doing what they know.

The three personalities every business owner contains:
- **The Technician** (the doer -- builds patios, installs fences)
- **The Manager** (the planner -- creates order, systems, predictability)
- **The Entrepreneur** (the visionary -- sees opportunity, drives growth)

At $1.4M, the CEO of a trade business is typically 70% Technician, 20% Manager, 10% Entrepreneur. At $3M, the ratio needs to flip to roughly 10% Technician, 30% Manager, 60% Entrepreneur. This is the core transition SecureWorks WA must make.

**For a trade business owner who IS the technician and needs to step back:**
1. Accept that your technical skill is not what grows the business -- systems do
2. Document every process you currently do from memory
3. Replace yourself in the field FIRST (you've started this with 2 lead installers)
4. Replace yourself in operations SECOND (remote ops manager is this step)
5. Replace yourself in sales/scoping LAST (2 scopers are this step)
6. Your job becomes: vision, strategy, relationships, and quality oversight

### 1.2 The Franchise Prototype Concept

Gerber's core prescription: **build your business as if you're going to franchise it 5,000 times.** This forces you to:

- Document every process so any competent person can execute it
- Remove owner-dependency from every function
- Create consistency that doesn't rely on individual talent
- Build a "turnkey" operation

**The Franchise Prototype rules:**
1. Provides consistent value to customers and employees beyond what they expect
2. Operated by people with the **lowest possible level of skill** (not lowest quality -- lowest required expertise through great systems)
3. A place of impeccable order
4. All work documented in **Operations Manuals**
5. Provides uniformly predictable service to the customer
6. Uses uniform colour, dress, and facilities codes

**What this looks like for an outdoor living construction company -- key processes to document:**

| Process | Current State (Typical) | Franchise Prototype State |
|---------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Lead handling | CEO/salesperson remembers to call back | Auto-response within 5 min, scripted qualification, booked into calendar within 24hrs |
| Scoping | Knowledge in scoper's head | Checklist + photo protocol + measurement template + digital tool (Secure Scope) |
| Quoting | Bespoke calculation each time | Standardised pricing matrix, automated quote generation, approval workflow |
| Materials ordering | Installer calls supplier from memory | PO system with approved suppliers, standard materials list per product type, AI ghost detection |
| Scheduling | Ops manager juggles calendar | Capacity planning system, crew assignment rules, buffer days built in |
| Installation | Lead installer's experience | Step-by-step build manual per product type, photo checkpoints, QC gates |
| Quality inspection | Owner spot-checks | Mandatory inspection checklist, photo documentation, sign-off before handover |
| Payment | Invoice when you remember | Automated milestone invoicing, payment terms enforced |
| Warranty | React when customer complains | Proactive 6-month and 12-month check-in, warranty register |
| Follow-up | Rarely happens | Automated review request, referral program, maintenance plan offer |

### 1.4 Australian Case Studies of E-Myth Transitions

#### Jim's Group -- The Ultimate Australian Franchise Prototype

Jim Penman started mowing lawns in 1982 with a **$24 investment** while completing his PhD. By 1989 he had sold ~100 mowing rounds. When competitor VIP Home Services arrived with 250 franchises, Penman began franchising "in self-defence."

**Key systemisation moves:**
- Created documented processes for every service type
- Built cloud-based scheduling and billing system (Jim's Jobs)
- Invested **several million dollars per year** in IT and systems
- Standardised training, customer service standards, and branding
- Adapted the franchise model to **50+ service industries** (including fencing, roofing, plumbing)

**Results today:**
- 5,700+ franchisees
- ~$1 billion annual turnover
- Operations across Australia, NZ, UK, Canada

**Relevance to SecureWorks WA:** Jim's proves that even simple trade services can be infinitely scalable when you systemise properly. The system is the product, not the service.

#### Hire A Hubby -- Systemised Trade Services

Founded in 1996, Hire A Hubby is Australia's largest residential handyman franchise with **280+ locations**.

**Key systemisation elements:**
- Cloud-based CRM allows franchisees to process quotes and book jobs within hours of first contact
- Instant text confirmation to customers when job request is processed
- Flat-fee model (not royalty-based) -- the more franchisees earn, the more they keep
- **Income Guarantee** for new franchisees (minimum weekly income while building)
- Two-week training course + "Forming Good Habits" ongoing program
- Bronze/Silver/Gold territory tiers for growth stages
- Supports solo operators through to multi-crew operations

**Relevance to SecureWorks WA:** Shows how a trade business can scale through systemisation without the complexity of a franchise model. The flat-fee structure and territory model could inform how SecureWorks structures its own crews.

### 1.5 How AI Changes the E-Myth Model

Gerber's original prescription was "write a manual." In 2026, AI fundamentally changes this:

**From Static SOPs to Dynamic Digital Guardrails:**
The most effective SOPs in 2026 are no longer PDF documents -- they are **interactive, dynamic workflows integrated into operational technology**. Leading solutions have transformed SOPs from passive paperwork into **active, sensor-triggered guidance systems**.

**Specific AI-SOP capabilities now available:**
- AI SOP generators observe tasks, identify workflow patterns, and generate structured documentation in real time
- Procedures automatically surface on mobile devices when specific conditions are detected
- SOPs can adapt to situations dynamically (e.g., if weather conditions change, the installation checklist adjusts)
- AI can reduce onboarding time by **60%** and boost efficiency
- Digital records create **immutable audit trails** for compliance and warranty claims

**What this means for SecureWorks WA:**
Instead of writing a 200-page operations manual that nobody reads, you can:
1. Use AI to generate initial SOPs from your existing processes
2. Embed SOPs into your existing tools (Secure Ops, Secure Trade)
3. Let AI surface the right checklist at the right time based on job type
4. Use AI annotations (already designed for your system) to flag deviations
5. Create adaptive decision trees that guide installers through edge cases

**The E-Myth principle still holds** -- you need documented, repeatable processes. But AI means those processes can be **living, adaptive, and self-improving** rather than static binders.

## 2. Alex Hormozi Frameworks -- Applied to Outdoor Living

### 2.1 The Grand Slam Offer Framework

From *$100M Offers*, Hormozi's **Value Equation:**

```
                Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                    Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice
```

To maximise value, you need to:
- **INCREASE** the Dream Outcome (make the result bigger/better)
- **INCREASE** the Perceived Likelihood (make them believe it will actually happen)
- **DECREASE** the Time Delay (deliver faster)
- **DECREASE** the Effort & Sacrifice (make it effortless for the customer)

### 2.2 Designing a Grand Slam Offer for Outdoor Living

**Current commodity offer:** "We build insulated patios. Here's a quote."

**Grand Slam Offer redesign:**

**Dream Outcome Enhancement:**
- Don't sell a patio -- sell "an extra room for your home that adds $30K-$50K in property value"
- Don't sell a fence -- sell "complete privacy and security for your family, professionally designed"
- Don't sell decking -- sell "your personal resort, 10 steps from your back door"

**Perceived Likelihood Enhancement:**
- Before/after portfolio with properties similar to theirs
- Video testimonials from Perth homeowners
- "See your patio before we build it" with 3D visualisation (Secure Scope)
- Transparent project timeline with milestone guarantees

**Time Delay Reduction:**
- "Quote within 48 hours" guarantee
- "Build starts within 2 weeks of approval" commitment
- Express installation options for standard designs
- Real-time project tracking through Secure Trade app

**Effort & Sacrifice Reduction:**
- Full design service included (no architect needed)
- Council approval handled for them
- All clean-up included
- Single point of contact throughout

### 2.3 Value Stack for Outdoor Living

Break the offer into visible components that stack perceived value:

The point is not to inflate prices -- it's to make the customer see the **total value** they receive, not just "a patio for $X."

### 2.4 Risk Reversal / Guarantees for Construction

Guarantees that work in construction (Hormozi says this is the #1 way to boost conversion):

1. **Price Lock Guarantee:** "Your quote is locked for 60 days. No surprise price increases, even if materials go up."
2. **Timeline Guarantee:** "If we don't start on the agreed date, we knock $500 off. If we don't finish within the agreed timeline (weather days excluded), another $500 off."
3. **Clean Site Guarantee:** "If our site isn't clean at the end of every work day, your next progress payment is reduced by $200."
4. **Quality Inspection Guarantee:** "Independent third-party structural inspection included at no cost. If anything doesn't meet Australian standards, we fix it at our expense before handover."
5. **Satisfaction Guarantee:** "If you're not 100% happy at the final walkthrough, we'll make it right before we ask for final payment."

**You can stack guarantees** -- Hormozi recommends using multiple guarantees covering different customer fears. For construction, the big fears are: will it cost more than quoted, will it take forever, will the tradies trash my yard, and will the quality be rubbish.

## 3. Cody Sanchez / Micro-PE / Contrarian Thinking

### 3.1 The "Boring Businesses" Thesis

Codie Sanchez's central argument: **the most valuable businesses are the ones people tend to ignore.** After nearly two decades on Wall Street, she realised the real path to wealth is in "unsexy" businesses that most overlook -- laundromats, car washes, plumbing companies, storage facilities, and trade services.

**Why trades are "boring" gold:**
- Essential services with recurring demand
- Baby boomer succession crisis: **58% of small businesses are owned by baby boomers**, and two-thirds have **no formal succession plan**
- 10,000 boomers retiring per day (US data, similar trend in Australia)
- Low competition from buyers -- most MBA graduates want tech startups, not fencing companies
- Stable cash flows, often 10-25% net margins
- Can be bought at 1-3x seller's discretionary earnings (SDE)

Sanchez has built a portfolio of **25+ businesses** generating eight figures in passive revenue.

### 3.3 Operator vs Owner Framework

Sanchez's framework for installing an operator and stepping back:

**Key principle:** "Find an operator first, then find a business to buy that they could run." The operator is MORE important than the business.

**The 3-6-12 Framework:**
- **3 months:** Deal-making education, operator identification
- **6 months:** Sourcing and negotiation
- **12 months:** Post-acquisition integration

**Finding an operator for a construction company:**
1. Look at pre-existing relationships (trust matters most)
2. Evaluate how they react to adverse situations (construction is full of them)
3. Consider existing employees -- the second-in-command who could step up
4. Align goals with equity/upside -- give them skin in the game

**For SecureWorks WA specifically:**
The remote ops manager is already positioned as the operator. The question is whether they have the capacity and authority to truly run day-to-day operations independently. Key decision: does the ops manager need to become local/on-site, or can remote work with the right systems?

### 3.4 Revenue Stacking -- Adding Recurring Revenue

Project-based businesses are valued lower because revenue is unpredictable. PE firms specifically seek **recurring revenue** in construction businesses.

**Recurring revenue streams for outdoor living:**

| Stream | Model | Estimated Revenue Potential | Effort |
|--------|-------|---------------------------|--------|
| Annual patio inspection & maintenance | $299-$499/year subscription | $50K-$150K/year at scale | Low - 1 person |
| Deck restaining & maintenance | $600-$1,200/job, annual | $30K-$80K/year | Medium |
| Fence maintenance & repair service | $199/year subscription | $20K-$60K/year | Low |
| Gutter/roof cleaning add-on | $199-$399/visit, seasonal | $20K-$50K/year | Subcontract |
| Outdoor living "care plan" bundle | $79-$149/month subscription | $100K-$300K/year at scale | Medium |
| Emergency repair service | Premium rates, as-needed | $20K-$50K/year | Variable |

**Why this matters for valuation:**
A $3M project-based construction company might sell for 2-3x SDE. Add $300K in recurring subscription revenue and the blended multiple could increase to 3-4x, adding **$300K-$900K** to company value on exit.

**Subscription structure best practices:**
- Offer 3 tiers: Basic, Standard, Premium
- Annual prepayment with discount (reduces cancellation risk, provides cash upfront)
- Track Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR)
- Use automated reminders, loyalty rewards, and service updates to retain subscribers

### 5.1 Core EOS Framework

Created by Gino Wickman, EOS is built on **Six Key Components:**

1. **Vision:** Where are you going? Who are you? (V/TO - Vision/Traction Organizer)
2. **People:** Right people in the right seats (Accountability Chart, People Analyser)
3. **Data:** Manage by numbers, not feelings (Scorecard with 5-15 KPIs)
4. **Issues:** Identify, discuss, solve (IDS process)
5. **Process:** Document and systematise your core processes ("The 6 to 20")
6. **Traction:** Execute with discipline (Rocks = 90-day priorities, L10 meetings)

### 5.2 Key EOS Tools for a Trade Business

**Level 10 (L10) Meetings -- HIGHLY RELEVANT:**

Weekly 90-minute leadership team meeting:
1. Segue/Good news (5 min)
2. Scorecard review (5 min) -- are numbers on track?
3. Rock review (5 min) -- are 90-day priorities on/off track?
4. Customer/employee headlines (5 min)
5. To-do list review (5 min) -- last week's commitments
6. **IDS -- Identify, Discuss, Solve (60 min)** -- the core of the meeting
7. Conclude (5 min) -- recap decisions, rate the meeting

**Rocks -- HIGHLY RELEVANT:**
90-day priorities (3-7 per person). For a trade business scaling to $3M, Q2 Rocks might be:
- Rock 1: Hire and onboard 3rd crew leader by end of quarter
- Rock 2: Document 10 core SOPs in Trainual/Whale
- Rock 3: Launch maintenance subscription program
- Rock 4: Implement QC inspection checklist for all jobs
- Rock 5: Achieve $280K/month average revenue

**Scorecard -- HIGHLY RELEVANT:**
5-15 weekly KPIs. For an outdoor living company:
- Leads generated this week
- Quotes sent
- Conversion rate
- Revenue booked
- Jobs completed
- Average job value
- Customer satisfaction score
- Crew utilisation rate
- AR days outstanding
- Warranty callbacks

**Accountability Chart:**
Not a traditional org chart -- defines WHO is accountable for WHAT. Every seat has 5 key roles. Eliminates "I thought someone else was handling that."

### 5.3 Is EOS Too Heavy for a $3M Company?

**Short answer: No, but you don't need all of it.**

EOS is designed for companies with **10-250 employees** and **$1M-$10M revenue**. SecureWorks WA at $3M is squarely in the sweet spot.

**Most valuable EOS tools for a trade business (implement these first):**
1. L10 meetings -- immediate impact on alignment and accountability
2. Rocks -- forces quarterly prioritisation
3. Scorecard -- moves from gut-feel to data-driven decisions
4. Accountability Chart -- clarifies who owns what
5. Core Process documentation -- the "how we do things here" system

**EOS tools that might be overkill at $3M:**
- Full V/TO exercise (keep it simple -- one-page strategic plan is enough)
- Complex People Analyser across all positions (useful for leadership only)
- Formal quarterly State of the Company meetings (important at $5M+)

### 5.4 EOS in Construction -- Case Study

**Ganneston Construction Corp** implemented EOS and reported:
- Improved project delivery: on-time and on-budget through better planning and execution
- Fewer surprises: weekly leadership meetings catch issues before they impact timelines
- Better communication between business development, operations, and construction teams
- Clearer accountability: from senior estimators to tradespeople, everyone aligned on priorities
- Structure: annual planning, quarterly reviews, weekly L10s, quarterly all-hands

### 5.5 EOS vs Simpler Alternatives

| Factor | EOS | Scaling Up (Verne Harnish) |
|--------|-----|---------------------------|
| Best for | $1M-$10M, execution focus | $5M-$100M+, strategy focus |
| Complexity | Simpler, prescriptive | More complex, flexible |
| Implementation time | Fast (weeks to months) | Slower (months to quarters) |
| Cost (self-implement) | $14 (book) | $30 (book) |
| Cost (with Implementer) | $50K+/year for 2 years | Similar |
| Focus | "Get things done" | "Think bigger, then do" |
| Best first step | L10 meetings + Rocks | One-Page Strategic Plan |

**Recommendation for SecureWorks WA:** Start with EOS. It's simpler, faster to implement, and directly addresses the current need (operational structure, accountability, execution). If the company reaches $5M+ and needs more strategic depth, Scaling Up can layer on top.

### 6.3 The Hub and Spoke Model

**Concept:** One brand (the hub), multiple service crews (the spokes), centralised operations.

**How it works for outdoor living:**
- **Hub (Central):** Brand, marketing, lead generation, quoting/estimating, admin, finance, customer service, quality standards, training, supplier relationships
- **Spoke (Crew):** Installation, on-site customer contact, daily reporting, safety compliance

**Advantages:**
- Crews can be added or removed without scaling the hub proportionally
- Standardised quality and customer experience across all crews
- Centralised purchasing power with suppliers
- One brand identity regardless of which crew does the work

**The hub doesn't need to grow linearly with spokes.** Adding a 4th crew doesn't require a 2nd admin or 2nd ops manager -- just better systems.

### 6.5 Subcontractor vs Employee Model in WA

**Legal framework in Western Australia:**

The Fair Work Ombudsman and WorkCover WA are clear: **getting this wrong can be very expensive.**

**Key classification factors:**
- **Control:** Do you direct how, when, where? (Employee) vs Worker determines this? (Contractor)
- **Integration:** Part of your business? (Employee) vs Separate business? (Contractor)
- **Equipment:** You supply? (Employee) vs They supply their own? (Contractor)
- **Delegation:** Must they personally do it? (Employee) vs Can they subcontract? (Contractor)
- **Payment:** Wage/salary with tax withheld? (Employee) vs Invoice per job? (Contractor)

**Critical WA-specific issue:**
In WA, both the principal AND the contractor are considered employers of the worker and are **jointly and severally liable** for workers' compensation. Having an ABN does not automatically make someone a contractor.

**Risk of misclassification:**
- Back-payment of superannuation
- ATO penalties
- WorkCover claims
- Fair Work unfair dismissal claims
- Payroll tax liability

**Pros of subcontractor model at scale:**
- Flexibility to scale up/down with demand
- No annual leave, sick leave, or redundancy obligations
- Access specialist skills as needed
- Pay for output, not hours

**Cons of subcontractor model at scale:**
- Less control over quality and schedule
- Higher per-unit cost than employees
- Legal risk of sham contracting
- Less loyalty and consistency
- Can't mandate training or processes as strictly

**Practical recommendation for SecureWorks WA:**
Keep crew leaders as employees (or very close to it) for quality control and consistency. Keep labourers as genuine subcontractors with proper contracts, their own ABNs, their own insurance, and the ability to work for others. Get legal advice -- this is a high-risk area in Australian construction.

### 7.2 Maintaining Build Quality at Scale (2 to 5 Crews)

**The quality problem:** When the owner was building every patio, quality was consistent because it was their personal standard. With 5 crews, that personal standard must become a system.

**Five-step quality control process for construction:**

1. **Pre-Construction:** Clear specs, material verification, crew briefing on standards
2. **In-Progress Inspection:** Daily/milestone checks with documented checklists
3. **Photo Documentation:** Every stage photographed with timestamp (before, during, after)
4. **Final Inspection:** Formal walkthrough with customer, checklist sign-off
5. **Post-Handover:** 30-day follow-up call, 6-month inspection, 12-month inspection

**Specific quality gates for outdoor living construction:**

| Stage | Quality Gate | Who Inspects | Documentation |
|-------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Site prep | Ground level, clearances, services location | Crew leader | Photos + checklist |
| Footings/posts | Depth, concrete mix, alignment, bracing | Crew leader + spot-check by ops | Photos + measurements |
| Frame/structure | Connection points, bolt torque, plumb/level | Crew leader | Photos mandatory |
| Roofing/panels | Flashings, fall, fixings, colour match | Crew leader | Photos |
| Electrical (if applicable) | Licensed electrician sign-off | Electrician | Certificate |
| Final finish | Clean-up, touch-ups, customer walkthrough | Crew leader + customer | Signed checklist |

**Key principle:** "Photograph all concealed work, especially at connection points and junctions. Include a tape measure in photos for scale." This creates a permanent visual record for warranty claims and future maintenance.

### 7.3 Photo Documentation System

**Requirements:**
- Timestamped photos at every quality gate
- Geo-tagged to confirm location
- Stored against the job record (not just on someone's phone)
- Accessible for warranty claims and disputes

**Options:**
1. **Built into Secure Trade app** (ideal -- crews already use it)
2. CompanyCam ($19/user/month) -- purpose-built for construction photo documentation

### 7.4 Warranty Tracking and Management

**Current best practice:**
- Log every job with completion date, materials used, crew, and warranty terms
- Automate warranty period tracking (most patio structures: 10-year structural, 15-year roofing panels)
- Set up proactive contact at 6 months, 12 months, and annually
- Maintain photo documentation as warranty evidence
- Track warranty callbacks as a quality KPI (target: <2% callback rate)

**Warranty as a competitive advantage:**
- Proactive warranty follow-up generates referrals ("They actually called US to check everything was fine")
- Annual inspections create touchpoints for upselling (deck restaining, maintenance plans)
- Documented warranty compliance protects against claims

## 9. Four Critical Systems for Scaling (Owned & Operated Framework)

From the Owned & Operated podcast, a scaling service business needs exactly four core systems:

1. **Structured intake system** -- consistent lead handling from first contact to quote
2. **Documented handoff processes** -- between every team and stage (sales > ops > crew > customer)
3. **Real-time operational visibility** -- not monthly reports; daily/weekly dashboards
4. **Repeatable workflows** -- new hires reach productivity in weeks, not months

**SecureWorks WA already has elements of all four:**
1. GHL handles lead intake (could be more structured)
2. Secure Ops handles handoffs (ops > crew via Secure Trade)
3. CEO dashboard provides visibility (Secure CEO)
4. Scoping tools provide repeatable quoting (Secure Scope)

**The gap:** The handoff from "lead comes in" to "job completes" has too many manual steps and human memory dependencies. The AI intelligence layer and annotation system (already designed but not fully built) would close much of this gap.
