### Licensing Insight (Important for Positioning)
Under WA Building Act 2011, **timber decking installation does NOT require a registered building contractor**. It's explicitly listed as excluded "builder work" alongside fire sprinklers, glazing, and partitioning. However:
- A building PERMIT is still required in most cases (>500mm high, >20m², <900mm from boundary)
- Projects over $20,000 require a registered building contractor
- Someone must still sign the permit application as "building contractor" and accept responsibility
- This means many deck builders operate as carpenters/tradespeople, not registered builders
- **Our positioning advantage**: We ARE a registered builder — adds credibility and compliance assurance

### A. TIMBER SPECIES — DETAILED COMPARISON

#### Jarrah (WA Native)
- **Durability**: Class 2, termite resistant, 25–40yr lifespan
- **Appearance**: Rich red to dark brown, ages to silver-grey if untreated
- **Maintenance**: Oil every 12–18 months to retain colour
- **Pros**: Local WA character, strong identity, easy to work with, little tannin leach
- **Cons**: Colour bleaches in harsh sun without maintenance, requires predrilling
- **Best for**: Heritage-style homes, premium builds, homeowners wanting local timber
- **Perth-specific**: Sourced locally, strong emotional connection for WA buyers

#### Spotted Gum (Premium)
- **Durability**: Class 1 hardwood, very dense/stable, 40+ years
- **Appearance**: Pale grey to deep brown with striking grain, "fiddleback" pattern
- **Maintenance**: Less than Jarrah, naturally weather-resistant
- **Pros**: Strongest durability, bushfire compliant (BAL-29), eco-friendly (plantation grown), minimal shrinkage
- **Cons**: Expensive, requires predrilling, can bow during installation
- **Best for**: Premium builds, bushfire zones, dry climates, high-traffic areas
- **Perth-specific**: Ideal for hills suburbs with BAL ratings

#### Merbau (Affordable Premium)
- **Durability**: Class 2, rot/insect resistant, 25–40yr lifespan
- **Appearance**: Reddish-brown to mahogany, darker than Jarrah
- **Maintenance**: Regular oiling, tannin bleed management required
- **Pros**: More affordable than Jarrah/Spotted Gum, extremely durable, easy to work with, minimal shrinkage
- **Cons**: HIGH tannin/oil content causes BLEEDING when wet (stains lighter surfaces like concrete, pavers, walls), imported (sustainability concerns from SE Asia)
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious premium builds, poolside decks
- **Perth-specific**: Very popular in Perth, but tannin bleed is a real issue on light-coloured paving — WARN CLIENTS

#### Blackbutt
- **Durability**: Class 2, fire-resistant (named after bushfire survival appearance)
- **Appearance**: Pale cream to pale brown, consistent colour, even grain
- **Maintenance**: Regular sealing essential (prone to cracking if unsealed)
- **Pros**: Fire-resistant (bushfire zones), easy to stain, dense/strong
- **Cons**: Requires regular maintenance, doesn't react well to adhesives, can surface check when painted
- **Best for**: Bushfire-prone areas, lighter aesthetic preference
- **Perth-specific**: Good option for hills suburbs where BAL compliance needed + lighter colour preferred

#### Ironbark
- **Durability**: Class 1, hardest timber available, 40+ years
- **Appearance**: Light brown to dark red, unique colour variation, character
- **Maintenance**: Minimal due to extreme hardness
- **Pros**: Strongest/hardest, fire/rot/termite resistant, often recycled (sustainable), commercial-grade
- **Cons**: Very hard to work with (requires predrilling), limited availability, expensive
- **Best for**: Commercial applications, high-volume areas, architectural projects
- **Perth-specific**: Often sourced recycled from old buildings — sustainability angle

#### Treated Pine
- **Durability**: Requires H3/H4 treatment for outdoor use
- **Appearance**: Light colour, accepts stain/paint well
- **Maintenance**: Regular staining/painting required
- **Pros**: Most affordable, flexible design (stain any colour), readily available
- **Cons**: Requires ongoing maintenance, susceptible to rot/termites without treatment, shorter lifespan (10–15 years)
- **Best for**: Budget builds, rentals, areas where aesthetics can be customized
- **Perth-specific**: H4 treatment recommended for ground contact in Perth's sandy soils

## 6. COUNCIL REGULATIONS — PERTH BUILDING PERMITS

### General Rule (Most Perth Councils)
A Building Permit IS required when:
- Deck is >500mm high from ground level
- Deck area >20m²
- Deck is <900mm from boundary
- Deck is >2.4m in height
- Pool deck (ALWAYS — safety/fencing regulations)

### Council-Specific Variations

**City of Wanneroo** — STRICTEST
- ALL decking requires Building Permit regardless of height/area

**City of Joondalup**
- Permit if >20m², >500mm high, <900mm from boundary, OR in bushfire-prone area

**City of Perth**
- Permit required for any construction/renovation/alteration

### Permit Types
- **BA1 (Certified)**: Plans certified by registered building surveyor before lodging, ~10 business days
- **BA2 (Uncertified)**: Standard application, longer processing

### Approval Timeline
- Standard: 2–4 weeks for straightforward cases
- Complex/neighbour consultation: 6–8 weeks
- Summer peak: Delays common — submit autumn/winter for faster processing
- Post-COVID delays: What was 2–3 weeks now commonly 6–8 weeks

### Permit Costs
- Typical: $135+ (varies by council + deck size/complexity)
- Fines for non-compliance: Minimum 3x permit cost, up to $50,000–$100,000

### Additional Requirements
- Setbacks: Minimum 900mm from boundaries, 1.8m from adjacent buildings
- Elevated decks (>1m high): Full compliance checks, structural certification, sometimes neighbour consultation
- Home indemnity insurance: Required if project >$20,000
- Notice of completion: Must be submitted within 7 days of completion (penalty $10,000 for non-compliance)

### Our Service Offering (Differentiator)
Position as "We handle the permit headache for you":
- Council lodgement included in service
- BAL assessment coordination
- Engineering certification for elevated decks
- Compliance documentation package

## 7. BUSHFIRE COMPLIANCE — BAL RATINGS EXPLAINER

### BAL Rating System (AS 3959:2018)

| Rating | Risk Level | Material Requirements |
|--------|-----------|----------------------|
| BAL-LOW | Minimal | Standard materials OK |
| BAL-12.5 | Low | Minor restrictions |
| BAL-19 | Moderate | Some restrictions |
| BAL-29 | High | Specific approved materials required |
| BAL-40 | Very high | Strict material/construction requirements |
| BAL-FZ | Flame Zone | Non-combustible ONLY |

### 2025 Updated Requirements (WA Building Amendment Regulations, April 2025)
- Ember-resistant materials required for Class 10a structures (includes decks)
- Minimum 900mm boundary setbacks, 1.8m from adjacent buildings
- Board spacing: 2–4mm recommended (5–10mm gaps particularly vulnerable to ember attack)
- Enclosed subfloor spaces required for decks in higher BAL zones
- Non-combustible or bushfire-resistant timber supports where subfloor unenclosed

### Timber Species Approved for BAL-29
Merbau (Kwila), Spotted Gum, Blackbutt, Turpentine, Red Ironbark, River Red Gum, Silvertop Ash, Jarrah (with treatment)

### Perth Suburbs Likely Requiring BAL Assessment
Hills suburbs: Kalamunda, Mundaring, Gooseberry Hill, Lesmurdie, Roleystone, Bedfordale, Darlington, Glen Forrest, Parkerville, Stoneville, Sawyers Valley, Hovea, Chidlow

### Coastal Area Considerations
- Stainless steel fixings required (316 grade) for salt corrosion resistance
- Composites + treated hardwoods recommended
- High-quality powder coat finishes essential
- Regular inspections: Every 3–5 years mandatory
- Relevant suburbs: Scarborough, Trigg, Hillarys, Sorrento, Burns Beach, Two Rocks, Mandurah foreshore

## 8. POOL DECKING — COMPLIANCE & REQUIREMENTS

### Mandatory Requirements
- Slip-resistant finishes mandatory around pools (AS 4586)
- Drainage must prevent pooling water beneath decks
- Balustrade height: Minimum 1000mm, gaps <125mm
- Stairs: Balustrade minimum 865mm above nosing
- Elevated >4m: No climbable elements between 150mm–760mm from floor
- ALWAYS requires building permit
- Must comply with pool fencing regulations (AS 1926)

### Best Materials for Pool Decks
1. **Composite (premium)**: Best option — slip-resistant, splinter-free, no maintenance, won't stain from chlorine/salt
2. **Merbau**: Popular timber choice, naturally rot-resistant, but tannin bleed issue near light surfaces
3. **Spotted Gum**: Premium timber option, handles moisture well
4. **HardieDeck**: Non-combustible cement board, excellent for pool areas, ultra-low maintenance

### Cross-Sell Opportunity
Pool deck + compliant pool fencing/balustrades:
- Glass balustrades: ~$320/linear metre
- Timber/wire balustrades: ~$180/linear metre
- Aluminium fencing: Mid-range option
